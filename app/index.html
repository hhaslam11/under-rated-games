Grand Theft Auto, Battlefield, Call of Duty... They're all good games and all; however, some games just deserve a little bit more love...

Here's a list of games that just aren't as popular & well known as they should be.

Shelter

Experience the wild as a mother badger sheltering her cubs from harm. On their journey they get stalked by a bird of prey, encounter perils of the night, river rapids crossings, big forest fires and the looming threat of death by starvation. Food is to be found, but is there enough for everyone?

Graphics

Shelter has a very unique hand-drawn-like art style to it.

Gameplay

Mostly interesting and interactive gameplay, with an even variety of puzzles, hiding from enemies, and trying to find food for your children. Sometimes gameplay can be a bit repetitive or frustrating, though.

Story

Shelter has a emotional linear story with an open world feel, different life-threatening events could happen, and depending on how well you play the game, your cubs will either live or die.

Shelter 2

Guide a powerful Lynx through vast open landscapes. Give birth to your cubs, raise and strengthen them by mastering your skills for hunting prey. Explore the wild beauty of nature and do everything possible to prepare your family for the vicious entities dwelling on the cold tundra.

Graphics

Shelter 2 Graphics are just like Shelter 1; Unique, yet beautiful

Gameplay

Shelter 2 focuses on an open-world gameplay, the goal is to keep your kittens alive in a fairly large world full of different threats/rewards.

Story

Shelter 2 took away most of the story aspect that Shelter 1 had, to make a more open-world game. There's very little story, other than short cut-scenes at the beginning and end of the game.

Paws

Paws is a standalone, single player, adventure, platformer set in the world of Shelter 2 with loss and friendship as its central themes.

Graphics

Once again, Paws doesn't disappoint with their one-of-a-kind art style, just like the previous two games.

Gameplay

Full of puzzles to solve and threats to avoid, Paws will keep you entertained throughout the entire (short) game.

Story

Paws goes back to Shelter 1's style with a linear story. It has an interesting, emotional, and suspenseful story.

Firewatch

Firewatch is a single-player first-person mystery set in the Wyoming wilderness, where your only emotional lifeline is the person on the other end of a handheld radio.

Graphics

Definitely one of the most gorgeous, atmospheric games on this list. Firewatch is worth playing just for the graphics itself.

Gameplay

Often boring gameplay, which mostly consists of walking back and forth from point-a to point-b. The game is open world, but there's not too much to explore. Same old trees and mountains throughout the entire map.

Story

Firewatch has a very suspenseful and exciting story, with great character development. However, the ending of the game was quite disappointing and uneventful.

Life is Strange

Life is Strange is an award-winning and critically acclaimed episodic adventure game that allows the player to rewind time and affect the past, present and future.

Graphics

Decent cartoon-like graphics, but nothing too special

Gameplay

Gameplay is interesting enough to not be considered boring, but sometimes the puzzles can be stretched out too long. Plenty of stuff to explore throughout the map of Arcadia Bay.

Story

I can not stress how amazing the story in this game is. Starts out with peaceful, typical high school drama, but gets really dark really fast. A ton of surprising twists in the story.

The Walking Dead Game by Telltale

A five-part Adventure Horror series set in the same universe as Robert Kirkman’s award-winning comic book series.

Graphics

Comic like graphics, but could be better

Gameplay

Often boring, long, and frustrating- but can also be fun and exciting at times.

Story

Another suspenseful, exciting, and emotional story. You never know what'll happen next; who'll die next; etc.

Prison Architect

Build and Manage A Maximum Security Prison.

Graphics

OK graphics, very simple, and sometimes look awkward/strange

Gameplay

A ton of events/situations to keep you entertained for ten's of hours. Building prisons is fun, and keeping your prisoners in control is even more fun.

Story

Has a pretty short campaign mode, but it's extremely boring and not even worth playing through. Stick to building your own prisons. Can't really expect much of a story from a Sims-like sandbox game, though.

A Story About My Uncle

A Story About My Uncle is a first person platforming adventure game about a boy who searches for his lost uncle, and ends up in a world he couldn’t imagine existed. Take help of your uncle’s mysterious inventions that let you jump incredibly high and far through beautiful scenery, uncover clues to your uncle’s whereabouts, and meet fantastical creatures that will help you on your journey.

Graphics

Average graphics for an indie game. Nothing special to see here.

Gameplay

Entertaining platformer, fun puzzles, and grappling hook! Can't go wrong with a grappling hook. Has time trails too, for after you finished the pretty short campaign.

Story

Fairly good, well told story. Nothing that'll leave you thinking about it though.

Tales from the Borderlands

Tales from the Borderlands is a five part episodic game series from the creators of The Wolf Among Us and The Walking Dead: A Telltale Games Series. Set on the unforgiving world of Pandora after the events seen in Borderlands 2, this is a story full of Borderlands’ trademark humor, following two adventurers on their quest for greatness.

Graphics

Unique cartoon-like graphics, but nothing too fancy.

Gameplay

Adventurous and entertaining, rarely ever boring.

Story

Amazing, action packed, twisted, dark, and humorous story.

SOMA

From the creators of Amnesia: The Dark Descent comes SOMA, a sci-fi horror game set below the waves of the Atlantic ocean. Struggle to survive a hostile world that will make you question your very existence.

Graphics

Pretty good, realistic graphics comparable to AAA games.

Gameplay

A lot of patience will be needed while playing this game, not a game that can be rushed. A large part of the game will be spent hiding behind any object you can find, but the creepy-atmosphere and suspenseful story keeps things interesting.

Story

It'll leave you questioning your entire existence and purpose. 'Nuff said.

7 Days to Die

7 Days to Die has redefined the survival genre, with unrivaled crafting and world-building content. Set in a brutally unforgiving post-apocalyptic world overrun by the undead, 7 Days to Die is an open-world game that is a unique combination of first person shooter, survival horror, tower defense, and role-playing games. It presents combat, crafting, looting, mining, exploration, and character growth, in a way that has seen a rapturous response from fans worldwide. Play the definitive zombie survival sandbox RPG that came first.

Graphics

Pretty disappointing graphics, often makes the world look strange, but the games still in Early Access- might change in the future

Gameplay

Zombies, guns, zombies, fully-destructible world, zombies, able to build/craft almost anything imaginable, zombies... and bees.

Story

No story, but that's how its designed. It tries to add a bit of a story-effect to it, but fails to make it sensible/interesting.
